College Branch Selection Counseling

Instructions

  • Before the counseling session, ensure that all necessary information about available branches, their eligibility criteria, and seat availability is provided to the candidates.
  • Explore various colleges, courses, and career opportunities with us. Get personalized counseling and guidance for your academic and professional journey.
  • Candidates should register for the counseling session and undergo document verification. They must submit all required documents such as mark sheets, identity proof, category certificate (if applicable), and counseling fee payment proof.
  • Conduct an orientation session to familiarize candidates with the counseling process, rules, and regulations. Provide information about the counseling schedule, seat matrix, and available branches.
  • Explain the seat allotment process to the candidates. It may involve rounds of seat allocation based on merit, category, and preferences filled by the candidates.
  • Candidates should be allowed to fill their preferred choices of branches and colleges based on their rank and eligibility. Provide guidance on selecting the right branch based on their interests, career goals, and academic performance.
  • Conduct mock counseling sessions to help candidates understand the process better. This can include simulating the seat allocation process and guiding candidates on making informed choices.
  • Depending on the number of candidates and available seats, multiple rounds of counseling may be conducted. Candidates should be informed about the schedule for each round and the procedure for exercising their choices.
  • After each round of counseling, announce the seat allotment result and display the list of allocated seats. Candidates should be informed about the next steps, such as reporting to the allotted college for admission.
  • Assist candidates in completing the admission formalities, such as payment of admission fees, document submission, and verification.
  • In case of vacant seats after the initial rounds of counseling, conduct waitlist rounds or spot counseling to fill the remaining seats.
  • Collect feedback from candidates about the counseling process to identify areas for improvement and enhance future counseling sessions.
